An Egyptian architect proposes to add a pool to the bottom third of a pyramid so that Cleopatra, Egypt's last Pharaoh, can have a relaxing bubble bath in the oasis in the afterworld. She requests that the pool should take as much as 30% of the pyramid's volume. The architect then sails all the way to the Pacific to retrieve a certain amount of pristine water from a nutrient-rich kelp forest. Unfortunately, he contracts malaria and dies. In a land far away, a Roman general named Marc Antony becomes smitten with the charms of the Egyptian queen and brings back the magical water filled with potent cell-renewing antioxidants. Since the construction team has built the base of the pyramid, he promises to gift Cleopatra her desired pyramid and pool. However, no one knows where the late architect stored the hieroglyphic papyrus that contains all the crucial measurements, such as the height of the pyramid. All they know is that the volume is V = (1/3) * base_area * height. Help Marc Antony figure out the height that the pyramid needs to be so he can finish building and impress the queen.

Input

The first line contains the volume of the cell-renewing elixir that Marc Antony brings back from the Pacific.

The second line contains the area of the base.

Output

Print out the height of the pyramid and the volume of the pyramid on two separate lines.

These two values should be rounded up using the round() function.

round(number, number of digits)

For example round(9.546, 2) = 9.55

If no number of digits is provided to the round function, it will round the number to the nearest integer.

For example round(9.546) = 10

In this problem, assume all outputs are whole numbers. Make sure you round the height first, print the rounded height, then use the rounded height to calculate the volume. The volume should also be rounded.

Sample Input

Volume of Elixer - 60
Area of the base - 10

Sample Output

Height of pyramid - 60
Volume of pyramid - 200
